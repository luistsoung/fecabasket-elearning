Level 1 · Start Coaching WABC

Mini-Basketball Animator

For instructors at basketball schools and U7-U11 youth coaches. The entry tier of the curriculum, focused on safety, fun and play-based fundamentals.

⏱ 30 hours 📱 20 h online 🏀 10 h on court 💵 FREE

Expected competencies

  • Ensure the physical and emotional safety of children
  • Promote the values: fair play, respect, fun
  • Run a Mini-Basketball session with play-based situations
  • Teach play-based fundamentals (ball handling, shooting)
  • Communicate with parents and the school/club

3 modules · 30 hours

1
Safety, prevention, first aid, values

Emergency protocols, hygiene, fair-play code, conflict management.

2
Mini-Basketball pedagogy and child development

Motor development stages, play-to-explanation ratio, managing a group of children.

3
Play-based fundamentals and games

Ball handling, dribble, pass, shooting: taught through games and challenges.

Assessment

Quiz per module (70% min.) · Final online MCQ · Filmed field challenge: run a 45-min Mini-Basketball session.

Level 2 · School-to-club transition

Monitor

The intermediate tier between Mini Animator and Level 1. Targets U10-U13 coaches bridging basketball school and competition. Consolidates fundamentals and introduces the first systems.

⏱ 45 hours 📱 30 h online 🏀 15 h on court 💵 FREE

Expected competencies

  • Consolidate individual fundamentals (dribble, pass, shooting)
  • Teach the basics of man-to-man defense
  • Introduce the first simple systems (give & go)
  • Prepare a team for regional youth competition
  • Manage the school-to-club transition
Level 3 · WABC Level 1

Level 1

For coaches of U12-U15, club or school teams. This level structures individual technical fundamentals and introduces man-to-man defense and transition.

⏱ 60 hours 📱 40 h online 🏀 20 h on court 💵 FREE

Expected competencies

  • Design and plan structured sessions across a cycle
  • Teach technical fundamentals (dribble, pass, shooting)
  • Install a man-to-man defense
  • Organize fast break and transition
  • Communicate effectively during a game
  • Understand FIBA rules and their application

6 modules · 60 hours

1Session design and cycle planning
2In-depth technical fundamentals
3Man-to-man defense U12-U15
4Fast break and transition
5In-game coaching (calls, time-outs)
6FIBA rules for the coach
Assessment

Quiz per module · Final exam with case study · Filmed 90-min session field challenge with written analysis.

Level 4 · WABC Level 2 / DETB

Level 2

For youth coaches in regional and national competition, or assistants in senior teams. Team tactics, zone defense, scouting and game preparation become the pillars.

⏱ 100 hours 📱 60 h online 🏀 40 h on court + camp 💵 FREE

Expected competencies

  • Master team tactics (offense, pick & roll)
  • Install a zone defense (2-3, 3-2)
  • Scout the opponent (video, statistics)
  • Prepare a game (game plan, adjustments)
  • Manage a tournament (prep, rotations)
  • Introduce adapted physical preparation
Level 5 · WABC Level 3

Level 3

For head coaches of elite teams, future regional DTNs, trainers of trainers. Periodization, leadership, multidisciplinary staff management and ability to train other coaches.

⏱ 150 hours 📱 80 h online 🏀 50 h + dissertation 💵 FREE

Expected competencies

  • Plan an annual season (periodization, training loads)
  • Lead an elite team
  • Build a multidisciplinary staff
  • Run a sport project (KPIs, reporting)
  • Train other coaches (knowledge transfer)
  • Conduct applied research (dissertation)
⚐ FIBA WABC Bridge

FECABASKET Level 2 and Level 3 graduates are eligible for the international FIBA WABC certification after a supplementary exam in front of a WABC instructor.

12 modules · 150 hours + dissertation

M1 · Annual periodization and planning
M2 · Leadership and roster management
M3 · Recruitment and scouting
M4 · Advanced physical preparation
M5 · Mental preparation and sport psychology
M6 · Multidisciplinary staff management
M7 · Sport KPI-driven management
M8 · Media and institutional communication
M9 · Methodology of coach training
M10 · Applied research and international monitoring
M11 · Case studies: great African and global coaches
M12 · Personal dissertation defense
Assessment

Quizzes · Personal dissertation (30-50 pages) · Oral defense before a jury (WABC instructors) · 5-day camp.
