Level 2Monitor · School-to-club transition

The intermediate tier between Animator and Level 1. Trains coaches in the school-to-club transition for U10-U13. Consolidates fundamentals and introduces the first systems.

FormatCapsules + MCQs + Quizzes
AudienceU10-U13 / Regional U13
PrerequisitesAnimator or 1 year of coaching

Level overview

The Monitor builds on the Animator's work by addressing the transition to competition. They consolidate the acquired fundamentals and introduce the first notions of team play. This is the coach who prepares a child to enter the regional U13 championship.

  • Consolidate individual fundamentals (dribble, pass, shooting) at a pre-competition level
  • Teach the basics of man-to-man defense (1v1, closing passing lanes)
  • Introduce the first simple systems: give & go, transition, 2-player game
  • Prepare a team for a U13 regional game (warm-up, time-out, rotations)
  • Manage the school-to-club transition and parent communication

1

Individual technical fundamentals consolidation

⏱ 12 hours

Module objectives

  • Stabilize the weak-hand dribble at full speed
  • Master the 4 pass types (chest, bounce, one-hand, over-the-top) at 5-7 m
  • Execute a lay-up on both sides with core control
  • Understand and apply 1- and 2-count FIBA stops

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

1.1 · Weak-hand dribbleIsolated work on the non-dominant hand, progressive speed
1.2 · The 4 passesChest, bounce, one-hand, over-the-top · choice by situation
1.3 · Left/right lay-upNo-step approach, knee drive, eyes on target
1.4 · Stops1-count simultaneous · 2-count alternate · pivot
1.5 · PivotPivot foot, offensive choice / ball protection
1.6 · Catching on the moveHands up, eyes on ball then on the area

2

Introduction to man-to-man defense

⏱ 10 hours

Module objectives

  • Understand the principles of man-to-man defense
  • Adopt the basic defensive stance (knees bent, arms wide, eyes on the belly)
  • Practice defensive moves (slides, sprint, closeout)
  • Close a passing lane without fouling

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

2.1 · Defensive stance"Ball-you-man-basket" triangle · 1 hand on ball, 1 on pass
2.2 · Defensive slidesWithout crossing legs · keep balance
2.3 · RecoverySprint to get back in front of your player
2.4 · Close the passing lane"Deny" · arm extended, back to the basket
2.5 · Help defenseFirst-line concept, without leaving your player too early
2.6 · Defensive reboundBox-out · shoulder-to-shoulder contact

Key drills

Stance

Lloyd Pierce defensive principles

Inspired by Lloyd Pierce "Basic Defensive Principles": work the basic stance and slides. Mirror drill: in pairs without a ball, 1 leads, 2 imitates.

3

First simple offensive systems

⏱ 12 hours

Module objectives

  • Teach the "give & go" as a collective fundamental
  • Run a fast 3-lane transition
  • Introduce the basic 2-player pick & roll
  • Understand spacing and ball movement

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

3.1 · Give & GoPass, cut, layup · timing and defender read
3.2 · Spacing4 to 5 m between players · open passing lanes
3.3 · Fast break3 lanes: point guard in the middle, wings on the sides
3.4 · Basic pick & roll2 players · screen on ball · defense read
3.5 · Ball movementPass then move · no static player
3.6 · DecisionsShoot / pass / dribble · the 3 options in 1 sec

Key drills

Give & Go

5-out offense and reading defense

From Joshua Longstaff "5 out offense, reading defence": reproduce the give & go inside the 5-out system. Pass, basket cut, catch, finish. 10 reps on both sides.

⏱ 8 min👥 Pair

Transition

Transition offense (Kestutis Kemzura)

Inspired by Kestutis Kemzura: organize the transition in 3 lanes. Work 3v0 then 3v2 — point guard in the middle, wings sprinting. Defender read to decide shot/pass.

P&R

Pick & Roll defense (Ron Adams)

From Ron Adams "Pick & Roll Defense": introduce P&R reads 2v0 then 2v2. Point guard dribbles off the big's screen, big rolls to the basket. Cover the 3 finishing options.

5-out spacing and ball movement

From Joshua Longstaff: conditioned 5v5 scrimmage — minimum 4 passes before a shot. Work on spacing (4-5 m between players) and continuous ball movement.

⏱ 15 min👥 5v5

4

U13 competition preparation

⏱ 11 hours

Module objectives

  • Prepare a U13 team for a regional championship
  • Set up a 15-min structured warm-up
  • Manage a game: lineup, rotations, time-outs
  • Communicate effectively with parents and school

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

4.1 · Warm-up10 min mobility · 5 min ball-in-motion
4.2 · Team lineup10 to 12 players · everyone's role · "everyone plays" rule
4.3 · RotationsQuarter by quarter · ensure balanced playing time
4.4 · Time-outMax 4 messages in 60 sec · 1 clear objective
4.5 · Post-gamePositive debrief · value effort over score
4.6 · ParentsParent code · pre-season meeting · spectator rules
