Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)

Have you been coaching for years without an official qualification? FECABASKET RPL turns your field experience into a WABC-recognized certification, without retaking the entire training. A fast-track route for experienced Cameroonian coaches. (French: Validation des Acquis de l'Expérience — VAE)

🎓 Official recognition

RPL in 3 key ideas

Your experience deserves a diploma. It just needs to be evidenced, structured and validated by a FIBA/DTN panel. Here is the principle.

🏀

1 · Experience as learning

Years spent on the court — practices, games, tournaments, youth development — constitute genuine training. RPL makes it official.

📂

2 · A structured portfolio

You submit a portfolio describing your career, clubs, teams and results. With certificates and concrete evidence attached.

⚖️

3 · An independent jury

A FECABASKET-DTN panel (FIBA trainers, WABC instructors) reviews your portfolio plus an oral interview. Final outcome: full validation, partial, or recommended top-up training.

👥 Target audience

Are you eligible for RPL?

RPL is for coaches with real field experience, even without an official diploma. Here are the criteria.

✅ Entry requirements

  • Minimum experience: 3 cumulative years of active coaching (clubs, schools, school teams, regional selections)
  • Documented activity: club certificates, competition rankings, federation letters, photos/videos of sessions
  • Age: 18+ for Animator · 21+ for Level 1 · 25+ for Level 2 · 30+ for Level 3
  • Citizenship: open to Cameroonians and foreign coaches practicing in Cameroon for 3+ years
  • Disciplinary record: no current FIBA/FECABASKET disciplinary sanctions

🛤️ RPL pathway

The 5 steps of the RPL process

From application to diploma award: a structured, supported pathway, taking 4-6 months on average.

1

Application and eligibility review

Duration: 2 weeks

Fill out the online form. Indicate: target level (Animator → Level 3), cumulative experience, context, motivation. The DTN checks your eligibility within 15 days.

2

Building the experience portfolio

Duration: 2 to 3 months · supported by a DTN mentor

You write a structured portfolio: career history, acquired skills, lived situations (key games, group management, season planning). Attach evidence: certificates, rankings, photos, videos.

3

Written portfolio assessment

Duration: 4 weeks · by 2 DTN examiners

The portfolio is independently read and graded by 2 FIBA/WABC examiners. They review consistency, pedagogical depth, and demonstrated skill level.

4

Oral interview with the panel

Duration: 1 day · in Yaoundé or by video conference

20-minute presentation + panel questions (5 members: 2 WABC trainers, 2 national team coaches, 1 DTN representative). You demonstrate your understanding of the game, your pedagogy, your vision.

5

Final decision and diploma award

Duration: 2 weeks after the interview

The panel issues one of three decisions: ✅ Full validation (diploma awarded) · 🔄 Partial validation (prescribed top-up training) · ❌ Reasoned refusal (redirected to standard training).

❓ Frequently asked questions

FAQ — FECABASKET RPL

How much does RPL cost?

Like the entire FECABASKET 2026/2027 training, RPL is 100% free. No application fee, no membership, no exam fee.

Can I aim directly at Level 3 through RPL?

Yes, if you can demonstrate significant experience in the national championship + proven results (titles, finals, national-team qualifications). The panel assesses the real level and may grant Level 2 or Level 3 directly.

What happens in case of partial validation?

The panel prescribes a targeted top-up pathway: typically 1-3 e-learning modules + 1 practical internship on the missing competencies. At the end, you obtain the full diploma.

Is the oral interview mandatory in person in Yaoundé?

No, the interview can be held by video conference (Zoom/Teams) for candidates outside Yaoundé. For coaches based abroad or in rural areas, that is the default option.

What evidence should I include in the portfolio?

At minimum: signed certificates from clubs/schools where you coached, historical FECABASKET licenses, game/tournament rankings, photos/videos of sessions or competitions. The richer the portfolio, the more precisely the panel can assess.
