Level 3Level 1 · WABC Level 1

The first WABC certifying level. For coaches of U12-U15, club or school teams. Builds technical fundamentals, man-to-man defense and session planning.

FormatCapsules + MCQs + Quizzes
AudienceU12-U15 / Club & school
PrerequisitesMonitor diploma or club experience

Level overview

Level 1 (equivalent to WABC Level 1) certifies a coach able to structure a youth team across a cycle. They master technical fundamentals, install solid man-to-man defense, and organize the fast break. They know FIBA rules and can coach in-game.

  • Design and plan structured sessions across a cycle (8 to 12 sessions)
  • Teach in-depth technical fundamentals (dribble, pass, shot, footwork)
  • Install a complete man-to-man defense with help and rotations
  • Organize the fast break and 3-/4-lane transition
  • Communicate effectively during a game (instructions, time-outs)
  • Master FIBA rules and their application in youth competition

Session design and cycle planning

⏱ 10 hours

Objectives

  • Build a balanced 90-min session (warm-up, technical, situation, scrimmage, cool-down)
  • Plan an 8-session cycle with measurable progression
  • Define SMART objectives for a cycle
  • Adapt content to the period (pre-season, competition, transition)

⏱ 12 hours

Objectives

  • Deepen dribbling techniques (changes of direction, hesitation, behind the back)
  • Master driving layups and outside shooting
  • Train offensive footwork (jab step, rocker step, eurostep)

Man-to-man defense U12-U15

⏱ 10 hours

Objectives

  • Install a strict man-to-man defense with clear principles
  • Teach help defense (first line, second help)
  • Practice 4- and 5-player rotations (full shell drill)
  • Train screen defense ("fight over", "switch", "hedge")

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

3.2 · On-ball defenseStance, hands, shot contest, charge vs no-charge
3.3 · Off-ball defenseHelp position · ball-you-man line
3.4 · CloseoutSprint then choppy steps · high hand on shot, low on drive
3.5 · Screen defense"Fight over" · "Under" · "Switch" · "Hedge"
3.6 · RotationsHelp the helper · X-out · verbal communication
3.7 · Taking a chargeFeet set, firm plant, keep balance

Fast break and transition

⏱ 10 hours

Objectives

  • Organize the fast break with 3 then 4-5 players
  • Install transition lanes and tempo
  • Decide quickly: pure transition or set offense
  • Manage defense-to-offense transition (outlet pass, first dribble)

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

4.1 · The outlet passRebound, pivot, wide-angle pass · the rebounder doesn't dribble
4.2 · 3 lanesCenter = point guard · wings = sprint · big = trailer
4.3 · The "3 or 4" ruleIf numerical advantage before the basket, finish in 3 or 4 passes max
4.4 · Drag screenTrailer screen if advantage is lost · entry into set offense
4.5 · Secondary breakContinuation to rebuild a structured offense
4.6 · Transition defenseImmediate "match-up" · no free player

FIBA rules for the coach

⏱ 10 hours

Objectives

  • Know the main official FIBA rules (2024 rulebook)
  • Understand sanctions: personal, technical, unsportsmanlike fouls
  • Master clock rules (24s, 14s, 8s, 5s)
  • Know how to challenge a call without earning a technical foul

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

6.1 · FIBA game format4 × 10 min · size 7 M / size 6 W ball · 3.05 m rim
6.2 · Clocks24-sec offense · 14-sec after shot/rebound · 8-sec backcourt · 5-sec inbound/FT
6.3 · FoulsPersonal · technical · unsportsmanlike · disqualifying
6.4 · The bonus5 team fouls per period · automatic free throws
6.5 · ViolationsTraveling, double dribble, backcourt, 3 seconds
6.6 · Time-outs2 in 1st half · 3 in 2nd · max 2 in the last 2 minutes
6.7 · SubstitutionsProcedure, signal at the scorers' table
6.8 · Coaching the refereeRespect, polite request, do not insist, technical foul consequences
