Level 1Mini-Basketball Animator

The entry tier of the FECABASKET curriculum, aligned with WABC Start Coaching. For instructors and coaches working with children aged 7 to 11. Focused on safety, fun and play-based fundamentals.

FormatCapsules + MCQs + Quizzes
AudienceU7-U11 / Mini-Basketball
PrerequisitesActive coach · 16+ years old

Level overview

The Mini-Basketball Animator is the first link in the training chain. They welcome children aged 7 to 11, introduce them to basketball through play, and ensure safety, fun and sporting values. At the end of this level, the learner will be able to:

  • Ensure the physical and emotional safety of a group of children aged 7 to 11
  • Design and run a ~60-min Mini-Basketball session
  • Teach play-based fundamentals: ball handling, dribble, pass, shooting, footwork
  • Apply first-aid protocols adapted to young athletes
  • Communicate effectively with parents, school and club
  • Promote FIBA values: fair play, respect, fun

1

Safety, prevention, first aid, values

⏱ 8 hours

Module objectives

  • Identify risks specific to a children's session (fall, contact, dehydration, asthma, allergies)
  • Implement a safety checklist before, during and after the session
  • Apply first-aid protocols: sprain, wound, fainting, head impact
  • Promote and enforce the fair-play code and FIBA values
  • Manage a conflict or a child's emotional crisis

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

1.1 · Before the sessionChecklist court, baskets, balls, hydration, first-aid kit
1.2 · Physical risksSprain, fall, contact, wet floor, fatigue, dehydration
1.3 · Emotional risksFear, exclusion, frustration, conflict, verbal aggression
1.4 · RICE ProtocolRest · Ice · Compression · Elevation on a sprain
1.5 · Head impactRecognize a concussion, immediate removal, alert parents/doctor
1.6 · Hydration and heatMandatory breaks, heatstroke warning signs
1.7 · Fair-play code5 FIBA values · respect the referee · respect the opponent
1.8 · Crisis managementCrying, refusal, scuffle · mediation, calming down

2

Mini-Basketball pedagogy and child development

⏱ 10 hours

Module objectives

  • Understand the motor and cognitive development stages of children aged 7 to 11
  • Design a session with an adapted play-to-explanation ratio (80/20)
  • Run a group of 12 to 20 children ensuring everyone participates
  • Apply the specifics of the FIBA Mini-Basketball rules (2.60 m basket, size 5 ball, no zone defense)
  • Adapt language and instructions to the learners' age

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

2.1 · The U7-U11 child profileMotor skills, attention, emotions, laterality
2.2 · The FIBA Mini-Basketball rules2.60 m basket · size 5 ball · key area · guaranteed playing time
2.3 · Typical session structureWelcome 5' · play-based warm-up 10' · content 35' · final game 10'
2.4 · Play / explanation ratio80% action · 20% instructions · the "30 seconds max" explanation rule
2.5 · The animator's languageCalm voice, simple words, demonstration over theory
2.6 · Group managementSubgroups, rotations, single-file lines limited
2.7 · Inclusion and fairnessEvery child must touch the ball, shoot, play · disability adaptations
2.8 · Formative assessmentObserve without judging · value progress · no ranking

3

Play-based fundamentals and games

⏱ 12 hours

Module objectives

  • Teach the 5 Mini-Basketball fundamentals: ball handling, dribble, pass, shooting, footwork
  • Turn each fundamental into a game to maintain attention
  • Differentiate between right- and left-handed children and train both hands
  • Set up a progression system over an 8-session cycle
  • Run a smooth 3-on-3 situational game

Detailed outline 💡 Click a topic to read the WABC manual content

3.1 · Ball handlingFamiliarization, ball on the floor, ball in the air, simple juggling
3.2 · DribbleStationary, advancing, weak/strong hand, head up
3.3 · PassTwo-hand chest, bounce, one-hand, on the move (the 4 Mini passes)
3.4 · ShootingBody position, eyes on target, adapted free throw
3.5 · FootworkDefensive slide, offensive sprint, 1-2 stops
3.6 · 1-on-1First duel: fake, acceleration, stop
3.7 · 3-on-3Mini format · mandatory-passes rule · no zone defense
3.8 · 8-session progressionCycle template · weekly objectives
