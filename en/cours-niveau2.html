Level overview

Level 2 (equivalent to WABC Level 2 / DETB) certifies a coach able to lead a team in competition. They master team tactics, combined defenses, scouting and game preparation. They can manage a tournament.

  • Master team offensive tactics (5-out, horns, motion, pick & roll)
  • Install a zone defense and an evolving defense
  • Scout an opponent and produce a structured game plan
  • Use game statistics to adjust in real time
  • Manage a tournament (preparation, recovery, rotations over 4 days)
  • Introduce players to adapted physical preparation
  • Handle the psychological dimension of a team in competition

Team offensive tactics

⏱ 12 hours

Objectives

  • Understand spread offense principles (5-out, 4-out 1-in)
  • Install a simple motion offense
  • Use named sets (Horns, Box, Stack)
  • Adapt the system to the roster profile

Detailed outline

1.1 · Spacing4 to 5 m between players · open driving lanes
1.2 · 5-Out motion5 players beyond the 3-pt line · cuts and screens
1.3 · 4-Out 1-In1 post inside · 4 perimeter · inside/outside two-man game
1.4 · Horns2 bigs at the elbows · P&R / P&P dual threat
1.5 · Box & StackSets to free a shooter or a post-up
1.6 · Quick hittersFast sets after time-out · 1 bucket in 6-8 sec
1.7 · Roster adaptationFast team vs size team · dominant player

Zone defense and evolving defense

⏱ 10 hours

Objectives

  • Master 2-3, 3-2, 1-3-1 zones
  • Understand when to switch to matchup zone
  • Identify the weaknesses of a zone and defend them
  • Practice the zone press (full-court defense)

Detailed outline

2.1 · Zone principlesCover areas, not players · constant communication
2.2 · 2-3 zoneProtects the paint · vulnerable to high post and 3-pt
2.3 · 3-2 zonePerimeter pressure · vulnerable to corners
2.4 · 1-3-1 zoneVery active · vulnerable at the edges
2.5 · Match-up zoneZone/man hybrid · frequent switches
2.6 · Zone press1-2-1-1 or 2-2-1 · pressure from inbounding
2.7 · Rebounding in zoneRebound triangle · cover positions

Screens: pick & roll, off-ball

⏱ 10 hours

Objectives

  • Master the central and side pick & roll
  • Differentiate roll, pop, slip based on defensive read
  • Install off-ball screens (down, flare, back, cross)
  • Learn screen defense (hedge, switch, ice, drop)

Detailed outline

3.1 · Central P&RTop point guard, ascending screen, 3 ball-handler options
3.2 · Side P&RStrong side · "ice" coverage forces baseline
3.3 · Roll vs PopBig rolls to the basket or pops to 3-pt depending on shooting
3.4 · SlipExit before contact if the defender anticipates switch
3.5 · Down screenDescending screen to free a shooter
3.6 · Flare screenSide screen to open a back shooter
3.7 · Cross screenHorizontal screen to reposition a big
3.8 · Screen defenseHedge, switch, drop, ice, blitz · choose by opponent

Detailed outline

4.1 · Scouting structureTrends · offensive sets · defenses · key players · ATOs
4.2 · Free toolsHudl basic · YouTube clipper · FastModel free trial
4.3 · Game selectionMinimum 3 recent games · different opponents
4.4 · Pattern identificationRepeated sets · post-TO play · end of game
4.5 · Player profileStrong hand, preferred dribble, weaknesses, emotional tendency
4.6 · Team presentation20 min · key clips · 3 main messages

Game preparation: game plan

⏱ 10 hours

Objectives

  • Build a structured game plan (1 double-sided page)
  • Prepare the pre-game week (D-3 to D-day)
  • Prepare ATOs (after time-out) and SLOB/BLOB (special situations)
  • Decide on the starting five based on the opponent

Detailed outline

6.1 · The game plan1 page · objectives · defensive keys · offensive keys · ATOs
6.2 · Typical weekD-3 scouting · D-2 walk-through · D-1 light + video · D-day game
6.3 · ATO designs3 sets for tight-score situations · 1 set for end of game
6.4 · SLOB & BLOBSideline and baseline inbounds · rehearsed sets
6.5 · Starting fiveBest 5 vs matchup-based · justify the choice
6.6 · In-game adjustmentsDecide at halftime · don't react in the heat

  • Manage the negative leader or "locker-room cancer"
  • Practice positive communication (feedback sandwich)

Detailed outline

9.1 · Tuckman modelForming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning
9.2 · CohesionEntry rituals · visual identity · shared values
9.3 · Distributed leadershipCaptain + area leaders · not everything on 1 player
9.4 · Team conflictMediation · 1-on-1 interview · clear rules
9.5 · The negative leaderIsolate · offer new responsibility · do not tolerate
9.6 · Constructive feedbackSandwich: positive · point of improvement · positive
9.7 · Confidence after a lossCold debrief · mental-recovery ritual
